# MIDCARE©

## Overview

MIDCARE© is an independent healthcare systems research framework developed by Sinéad Madden through the Hybrid Simulation Modelling Lab (HSML) and research at the University of Limerick.

The framework applies:

* healthcare demand analysis,
* critical mass analysis,
* System Dynamics Modelling (SDM),
* and whole-system planning approaches

to examine regional healthcare capacity, Emergency Department (ED) overcrowding, workforce pressures, and long-term healthcare system behaviour within Ireland’s Mid-West region.

MIDCARE© explores how population growth, hospital capacity constraints, consultant workforce dynamics, delayed discharge pathways, and healthcare system feedback interact over time to influence congestion and regional healthcare performance.

---

## Research Focus

Key areas examined include:

* Emergency Department overcrowding
* Acute hospital capacity planning
* Regional healthcare inequality
* Consultant workforce pressures
* Model 2 and Model 4 hospital interaction
* Whole-system healthcare planning
* Feedback-driven healthcare congestion
* Group Model Building (GMB)
* Strategic healthcare simulation modelling

---

## Modelling Approach

The framework combines:

### Critical Mass Analysis

Illustrative Emergency Department attendance threshold analysis associated with sustainable emergency care delivery.

### System Dynamics Modelling (SDM)

Feedback-based simulation modelling examining how congestion emerges endogenously through interactions between:

* population growth,
* inpatient capacity,
* workforce constraints,
* throughput limitations,
* delayed discharge pathways,
* and regional healthcare system feedback.

### Whole-System Planning

An integrated systems approach recognising healthcare systems as interconnected regional structures rather than isolated facilities.

## Important Notes

* MIDCARE© is an academic and policy research framework.
* The work is exploratory and intended to support evidence-informed discussion and healthcare planning.
* Simplified calculations included within this repository are illustrative only and should not be interpreted as predictive forecasts.
* SDM outputs represent simulated system behaviour under stated assumptions rather than deterministic predictions.
